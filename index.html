Exactly How Good of a Player is Steph Curry?

By: Anant Patel

Prelude:

Pronunced by many players and analysts as the greatest shooter of all time, Stephen Curry is a professional basketball player who currently plays on the Golden State Warriors. Drafted in 2009 in the first round, Curry has been an astonishing player ever since he stepped foot on an NBA court. It is said by many that he changed the game of basketball, given his ability to consistently make shots from almost anywhere on the court. His accomplishments are many, but his most striking one is unanimously winning the Most Valuable Player (MVP) in the years 2015-2016. My goal is to do some metrics to see how much he has improved since he was a rookie, as well as compare him to NBA league averages to get a grasp of his shooting level.

Now, we want to include an additional stat that is not in our current dataset, called 'Efficiency'. Efficiency in basketball is a measure of an individual player's efficiency in a game. It was created by statistician Martin Manley, and is derived from the equation shown in the code below. A more comprehensive metric for calculating efficiency is called the player efficiency rating (PER), but the calculations are too complex, and we would need more data in order to compute it. More information can be found at https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Efficiency_(basketball)#:~:text=Individual%20player%20efficiency%20is%20expressed,Missed%20FT%20%2D%20TO)%20%2F%20GP.

In [165]:
# Adding the efficiency column to Steph Curry's stats
# Efficiency formula: (PTS + REB + AST + STL + BLK  Missed FG  Missed FT - TO) / GP

efficiencyArr = []
for index, row in stats.iterrows():
    pts = row['PTS']
    reb = row['REB']
    ast = row['AST']
    stl = row['STL']
    blk = row['BLK']
    missed_fg = row['Total Shots'] - row['Successful Shots']
    missed_ft = row['Total FT'] - row['Successful FT']
    to = row['TO']
    efficiency = pts + reb + ast + stl + blk - missed_fg - missed_ft - to
    efficiencyArr.append(efficiency)

stats['Efficiency'] = efficiencyArr

Now, our data is cleaned up and ready to be represented.

Lets use three regression models, one for each graph above, and see if we accept or reject the null hypothesis of no linear relationships between Stephen Curry's statistics over time.

Part 5: Insights Attained

There are a few takeaways that we can gather from this analysis on Steph Curry as a player. We can see that Curry has definitely improved over his years in the NBA, and that his progression follows a linear growth. We can also takeaway the fact that Curry is much more efficient at shooting three pointers than the rest of the league. Finally, we have a reason to further investigate Curry's impact on the entire league's willingness to shoot more three pointers. All in all, we can conclude that Stephen Curry is an elite player who continues to dominate whenever hes on the court, and will leave an impact on the NBA even after he retires.
